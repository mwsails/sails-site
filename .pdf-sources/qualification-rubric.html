The Velocity Qualification Rubric.

Five binary questions, scored in under a minute, applied to every deal with zero exceptions. The scorecard that keeps junk out of your pipeline and fiction out of your forecast.

4 pages · By Matt Weisman

The Five PointsPage 2 of 4

Five points. Score 0 or 1. Evidence only.

Every point needs a sentence the buyer actually said. If the CRM note can't quote it, the point wasn't earned.

1

Trigger · Why this conversation, why now?

Earns the point: the buyer names a specific event (hired reps, lost a deal, missed a quarter, churned off a competitor). Scores zero: "they seemed interested," no event named. No trigger means no urgency.

2

Fit · Are they actually your ICP?

Earns the point: verifiable facts match your ICP definition (size, motion, industry, use case). Scores zero: "they could probably use it." Out-of-ICP wins churn in month four.

3

Authority · Can this person buy?

Earns the point: your contact owns the decision, or has booked the owner into the next call. Scores zero: "I'll share it with my boss." Booked beats promised, every time.

4

Cost of status quo · What does doing nothing cost, in their words?

Earns the point: the buyer states a cost ("we lose about two deals a month because nobody follows up"). Scores zero: your pitch reflected back, or no cost named. If they can't say it, they won't fight for the purchase.

5

Path · Is there a buyer-committed next step with a date?

Earns the point: a demo, decision meeting, or pilot scoping on the calendar, committed by the buyer. Scores zero: "I'll get back to you." Rep motion is not deal motion.

Scoring & CheckpointsPage 3 of 4

What the score decides.

ScoreVerdictWhat to do
5 / 5Qualified, hotAdvance now. Demo booked before the call ends. Cycle target inside 14 days.
4 / 5QualifiedAdvance to demo. Note the missing point; close it on the demo call.
3 / 5DevelopExactly one targeted action against the specific gap, with a deadline. Doesn't close in one touch: it's a 2.
0-2 / 5Not qualifiedNurture track with a revisit date, or a polite no. Does not enter pipeline. This is a win: you just saved six weeks.

The number that matters most is the 3. "Develop" is a state with a timer on it, not a parking lot. One gap-closing action, then up to a 4 or out of the pipeline.

The three checkpoints

  • End of discovery. Score in the last two minutes of the call while the evidence is fresh. The five points map to the five discovery questions.
  • Entry into pipeline. No deal enters the CRM as an opportunity below a 4. This one rule does more for forecast accuracy than any forecasting method.
  • Requalification of stalled deals. When the weekly pipeline review flags a deal past its time-in-stage benchmark twice, run the rubric again on current evidence. Triggers expire. Authority changes. The rubric is how you notice.

Making It StickPage 4 of 4

The failure modes to watch.

  • Scoring hope instead of evidence. The fix is the phrase "in their words." Every point needs a quotable sentence from the buyer in the CRM note.
  • Rubric drift. Reps under quota pressure round 3s up to 4s. The counter is public scoring: the score is said out loud in the pipeline review when the deal enters, and anyone can challenge a point.
  • Treating nurture as failure. A 2 with a real trigger arriving next quarter is future pipeline. Move it to nurture with a revisit date and it comes back warm. Force it in now and it burns 20 rep-touches dying slowly.

Install checklist

  • ICP definition written and agreed (fit has to be checkable against something)
  • Five score fields added to the CRM opportunity record
  • Entry rule active: no opportunity below a 4
  • Score spoken aloud in the weekly pipeline review when each deal enters
  • Stalled deals requalified with the same rubric, not gut feel
