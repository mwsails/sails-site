01 · The Problem

Why Most Early Sales Teams Stall

Velocity sales is a B2B motion built around short cycles, transactional buyers, and high-volume rep activity. ACVs run $2,000 to $24,000. Deal cycles close inside 90 days, often under 14. Reps work as BDR/AE pairs or full-cycle inside sales, and the team's job is to move volume through a documented playbook. It's the opposite of enterprise sales, where six-figure ACVs and 12-month cycles reward MEDDIC, multi-threading, and committee navigation.

You're closing deals, but it's still the founder doing it, or one rep out of three is carrying the number. Every deal still feels like the first one. There's no process to hand anyone.

Or you have a team in place, some results, but you can't explain why two reps are hitting and two aren't. Coaching is happening, but it's really just pipeline inspection.

The problem isn't effort. It's that velocity sales has its own physics. Short cycles, transactional buyers, BDR/AE economics, and nobody built you an engine that matches how your buyers actually buy.

Flat pipeline. No momentum. No system.

02 · The Engagement

Assess. Build. Coach.
The 8-Week Engine.

Before you can Always Be Closing, you need to Assess, Build, Coach.

Two tracks · One engine

Build track

For founder-led teams with wins but no system. You've closed deals, but the process lives in your head. We extract it, document it, and make it teachable before your first AE hire.

Scale track

For teams with reps in seat but uneven results. Some are hitting, some aren't. We diagnose the funnel, the rep distribution, and the coaching cadence, then rebuild what isn't working.

Assess

Weeks 1–2 · Track-specific

Diagnosis before prescription.

Build track gets a GTM Foundations Brief (ICP, positioning, motion fit). Scale track gets an Audit Report with 3 to 5 prioritized quick wins. No assumptions. No generic frameworks applied before we know what's actually broken.

Build

Weeks 3–6

The complete velocity sales engine.

A 20–30 page Sales Playbook built for your motion, including:

  • ICP definition and qualification rubric
  • Messaging framework and positioning
  • Pipeline stages with exit criteria
  • Discovery and demo frameworks
  • Outbound sequence design
  • Meeting cadence and KPI tracker
  • Hiring profile and org structure

Coach

Weeks 5–8

Implement, course-correct, make it stick.

Bi-weekly sessions during and after the Build phase, so nothing sits in a doc. Async support between sessions for quick reviews and gut-checks.

This is an async-first engagement. Most of the work happens between sessions, not in meetings. You get a founder with 8 years of operator experience, not a slide deck and a handoff.

06 · FAQ

Common Questions

What's the difference between the Build track and the Scale track?

Build track is for teams with wins but no system: typically founder-led sales that needs to be handed off. Scale track is for teams with reps in place but uneven results. We determine which fits during the discovery call.

Do I need to have a sales team in place?

No. Many Build track clients are pre-first-hire. We build the engine before you hire into it.

How much time does this require from my team?

The engagement is designed to be async-first. You'll need to complete a diagnostic questionnaire before kickoff and commit to bi-weekly 60-minute sessions during the Coach phase. Outside of that, the work is mine.

What happens after week 8?

Coaching retainers are available starting at $2K/month for continued bi-weekly sessions and async support. No obligation. It's an option, not the default.

Is this right for a non-SaaS business?

Possibly. The frameworks are built around B2B SaaS velocity motions, but the underlying physics apply to any short-cycle, transactional B2B sale. Reach out and we'll be direct about fit.
